Nikola Tesla

The inventor of alternating current (AC) and the induction motor

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist who is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system.

Here is a time line of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austria
  • 1875 - Enrolls at Austrian Polytechnic
  • 1882 - Begins working for Continental Edison
  • 1882 - Identifies Magnetic Induction Field Principle
  • 1884 - Arrives in the United States
  • 1885 - Quits work for Continental Edison
  • 1886 - Forms Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing Company
  • 1882 - Forms Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing Company
